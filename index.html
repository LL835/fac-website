About me

Background

My name’s Derek, and I graduated in 2019 with a degree in Law with American Legal Studies. I’m currently working in the editorial department of a legal publishing company in London. My responsibilities include copy editing and publishing digital content on the company's website.


Outside of work, I like to code. I am currently learning Python, HTML, CSS, and Javascript with the ultimate goal of becoming a full-stack web developer. I’m interested in art and design and spend a lot of my free time sketching characters. I also like reading and reality TV.

Why web development?

I decided to pick up coding as a hobby because I really wanted to learn a new skill. Coding felt like the right choice because I had dabbled in it before when I was in school and really enjoyed it. It also seemed like a really practical choice given how technology-driven the workplace is, and I thought it might come in handy at some point. Now, having practised for several months, I'm ready to take the next step and start working towards a career in web development.


What I like most about web development is that it requires creativity and problem-solving. Being able to build something from scratch and say “hey, look at what I made!” is really rewarding. I also really like designing websites, and I often spend a lot of time playing with layout, colours, and fonts.

Why Founders and Coders?

Because even though I like self-learning, it can also be really overwhelming at times. Going to a bootcamp where I can learn from others in a dedicated training environment is exactly what I need to jump-start my career change. The fact that there’s the possibility of coming out of the process with an apprenticeship is definitely a plus too.
